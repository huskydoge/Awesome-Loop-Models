# Daily Loop-Model Briefing — 2026-05-14

## Outcome

One very recent paper was added to the main collection: **Recurrent Transformer-Based Near- and Far-Field THz Wideband Channel Estimation for UM-MIMO** (`2605.12578`). The key inclusion evidence is unusually explicit for an application paper: the authors train **one transformer block with state memory** and then **iteratively apply** that block for hybrid near/far-field channel estimation.

## Included paper

### Recurrent Transformer-Based Near- and Far-Field THz Wideband Channel Estimation for UM-MIMO

This paper targets THz ultra-massive MIMO channel estimation, where users may occupy both near-field and far-field propagation regimes and hybrid beamforming limits digital-chain observations. The proposed model is a block-recurrent transformer: instead of stacking many independent transformer blocks, it trains a single state-memory transformer block once and applies it repeatedly during estimation.

Why it was added: the abstract clearly states same-operator reuse — "a single transformer block equipped with state memory can be trained once and then iteratively applied" — satisfying the repository's strict loop-model rule. The paper is classified as an **Applications Focused** entry because the main reader takeaway is a concrete wireless channel-estimation use case, with reported gains of about **5 dB NMSE** in narrowband and **7.5 dB NMSE** in wideband scenarios over state-of-the-art baselines.

## Watchlist and near-misses

### Local Inverse Geometry Can Be Amortized

This remains the strongest same-day adjacent hit. It learns a reusable reverse operator inside Deceptron and deploys it in D-IPG, an iterative inverse-problem solver. The loop is real: each solver step maps a residual-corrected measurement proposal back to latent space and updates the current estimate.

Why it was not added: the repeated computation is framed as an external solver trajectory for inverse problems, not as a learned layer/block/operator reused inside one model forward process. That makes it useful watchlist material, but not a conservative main-list addition under the repo's strict loop-model definition.

### KV-Fold: One-Step KV-Cache Recurrence for Long-Context Inference

KV-Fold treats the KV cache as an accumulator over sequence chunks. A frozen transformer processes each new chunk conditioned on the accumulated cache, appends new keys and values, and passes the enlarged cache forward. The reported behavior is stable across long chains and preserves needle-in-a-haystack retrieval up to 128K tokens.

Why it was not added: the recurrence is implemented as repeated chunk-level forward passes through a frozen model. It is relevant to cache recurrence and long-context systems, but the abstract does not make a same-forward reused internal learned block central.

### Inline Critic Steers Image Editing

Inline Critic adds a learnable token that critiques a frozen image-editing model at intermediate layers and steers subsequent hidden states during the ongoing forward pass. This is a useful example of within-forward refinement rather than post-hoc correction after full generation.

Why it was not added: the mechanism is an intervention token over ordinary model depth, not an explicit looped, weight-tied, recurrent-depth, or fixed-point operator.

### Elastic Attention Cores for Scalable Vision Transformers

VECA uses learned core tokens as a communication interface between patches, propagating those cores across layers and allowing compute/accuracy trade-offs along the core axis.

Why it was not added: the paper propagates learned state across layers, but the abstract does not claim reuse of the same learned layer, block, or operator. It is an adjacent efficient-attention design, not a strict loop model.

### ATLAS: Agentic or Latent Visual Reasoning? One Word is Enough for Both

ATLAS introduces functional tokens that serve both as agentic operations and latent visual reasoning units, preserving compatibility with standard SFT/RL training.

Why it was not added: the abstract describes token-level operations and latent reasoning, but not repeated reuse of a learned internal layer, block, or fixed-point operator within one forward process.

### Deep Learning for Solving and Estimating Dynamic Models in Economics and Finance

This same-day exact-phrase hit mentions "Deep Equilibrium Nets," but in the context of embedding economics equilibrium conditions into neural-network loss functions.

Why it was not added: the relevant equilibrium is an economics/modeling constraint, not a DEQ-style implicit layer or shared learned operator repeatedly applied to reach a neural fixed point.

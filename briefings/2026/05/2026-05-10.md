# Daily Loop-Model Briefing — 2026-05-10

## Outcome

No newly announced paper was added to the main collection today. The current arXiv slice contained several papers with loop-adjacent vocabulary — latent reasoning, implicit functions, agentic iteration, and recurrent dynamics — but none of the newly announced hits clearly satisfied the repository rule that a learned internal operator is reused within a single forward process.

## Backlog candidate for separate review

**Visual Implicit Autoregressive Modeling (VIAR)** is the strongest high-confidence item surfaced by the exact-phrase sweep. It introduces a next-scale visual autoregressive generator with an implicit equilibrium layer placed between shallow pre/post blocks. The paper reports Jacobian-Free Backpropagation, a per-scale inference iteration knob, and fixed-point convergence; on ImageNet 256×256 it reports FID 2.16 with 38.4% of VAR's parameters, plus memory/throughput trade-offs controlled by the iteration budget.

Why it is in scope: the implicit equilibrium layer is solved through repeated fixed-point iterations over the same learned operator, matching the repo's `implicit-layer` loop mechanism. Why it was not added today: it was published on 2026-05-02, so it is better handled as a separate backlog/backfill decision rather than mixed into this daily watch slice.

## Checked but not added

- **LatentRAG** moves reasoning and retrieval into latent tokens for efficient agentic RAG, but the abstract describes direct latent-token generation and retrieval alignment, not repeated reuse of one learned internal block.
- **Visual Latents Know More Than They Say** optimizes visual latent reasoning at inference time with a frozen backbone. This is useful latent-reasoning context, but the loop appears to be optimization over latents rather than a loop-model architecture.
- **Spatiotemporal Hidden-State Dynamics as a Signature of Internal Reasoning in LLMs** analyzes hidden-state transitions across tokens and layers during decoding. It is about reasoning diagnostics, not a recurrent-depth or tied-operator model family.
- **How Long Does Infinite Width Last?** studies signal propagation in ordinary linear recurrent sequence models as recurrence length grows with width. Sequence-time recurrence is explicitly outside the curated loop-model scope unless it is repurposed as same-forward depth/compute reuse.
- **Automated alignment is harder than you think**, **MAS-Algorithm**, and **Differentiable Parameter Optimization for DAEs with State-Dependent Events** were false positives from shared-weight, algorithmic, or implicit-function wording and were not loop-model candidates.

# Daily Loop-Model Briefing — 2026-05-12

## Outcome

Three recent arXiv papers were added to the main collection. Each was verified on its exact arXiv abstract page and each makes the reused-operator loop central rather than relying on generic “iterative” language.

## Added to the collection

### Solve the Loop: Attractor Models for Language and Reasoning

Attractor Models start from a looped-transformer motivation but move the recurrent computation into an attractor module. A backbone first proposes output embeddings, then the attractor module solves for a fixed point; gradients are obtained through implicit differentiation, so effective depth can grow without storing all unrolled states.

Why it is in scope: the paper is explicitly about replacing fixed-depth recurrence with a repeated fixed-point solve over a learned attractor operator for one prediction. It fits both flat-loop and implicit-layer mechanisms and belongs under Architecture and Algorithm Designs because the central contribution is a new loop-model architecture.

Main reader takeaway: the abstract reports Pareto improvements over standard Transformers and stable looped models for language modeling, including up to 46.6% perplexity improvement and up to 19.7% downstream accuracy improvement, plus small-model reasoning results such as 91.4% on Sudoku-Extreme and 93.1% on Maze-Hard.

### Sparse Layers are Critical to Scaling Looped Language Models

This paper studies why dense looped language models can scale worse than standard transformers and shows that sparse MoE layers change the picture. Because the same looped layers are revisited, different experts can be routed on different passes through shared layers, increasing effective expressivity without adding a unique parameter set per depth.

Why it is in scope: looped language models repeat transformer layers through depth within one prediction, and this paper directly analyzes how sparse layers and early exits behave at those loop boundaries. It is categorized as Architecture and Algorithm Designs because it gives a concrete scaling recipe for looped LMs.

Main reader takeaway: the abstract reports that Looped-MoE models scale better than dense looped models and can beat standard transformers at scale, while loop boundaries provide stronger early-exit points because they use the same layers that produce the final output.

### Quantum Injection Pathways for Implicit Graph Neural Networks

This paper extends graph deep-equilibrium models by comparing where a quantum signal enters the fixed-point computation. The variants hold the quantum signal fixed, recompute it from the current iterate, or recompute it from the classical backbone output; the paper then derives contraction conditions and compares solver behavior on graph-classification benchmarks.

Why it is in scope: DEQs define their output as the fixed point of a learned operator, so the repeated fixed-point solve is central rather than incidental. This paper keeps that implicit-layer mechanism at the center and studies how new signal pathways affect the looped graph operator.

Main reader takeaway: the abstract reports that independent quantum injection gives the best test accuracy on NCI1, PROTEINS, and MUTAG while requiring fewer forward-solver iterations than the classical equilibrium baseline and dependent quantum variants.

## Checked but not added

- **Latent Chain-of-Thought Improves Structured-Data Transformers** is close to the repo boundary and worth watching: it studies latent CoT, depth, looping, and a looped-transformer baseline for structured data. The proposed recurrent CoT mechanism, however, is described as processing the structured-data transformer again after appending feedback tokens, so it reads more like repeated full-model passes than a canonical main-list loop-model architecture.
- **A Single-Layer Model Can Do Language Modeling** proposes a recurrent language model with one state vector revisited at every token step. That is interesting recurrent sequence modeling, but ordinary sequence-time recurrence is outside the repo's depth-reuse scope by itself.
- **ELF: Embedded Language Flows** is a strong adjacent language-generation paper, but diffusion/flow iterative generation is explicitly outside the main loop-model boundary unless it connects directly to reused internal layers inside one model forward process.
- A same-day visual-latent-reasoning cluster — including **Fill the GAP**, **SCOLAR**, and **UniVLR** — was also checked. These papers focus on latent visual tokens, alignment, and compact reasoning workspaces around multimodal LLMs; they stay on the watchlist unless a clearer same-forward reused learned block appears in the paper text.

# Daily Loop-Model Briefing — 2026-05-04

## Outcome

No new paper was added to the main collection today. The watch found several fresh papers around latent reasoning, but none crossed the repository’s strict boundary: for one prediction, a learned internal layer, block, module, or operator should be reused within the model’s forward process.

## Strongest watchlist signal

**State Stream Transformer (SST) V2** is the most relevant item to watch. The paper introduces a state stream at each decoder layer, produced through an FFN-driven nonlinear recurrence and blended horizontally through the sequence. It also reports optional latent deliberation before emitting a token, with a claimed +15.15 point gain over a fine-tuning-matched baseline on GPQA-Diamond and a 46% reduction of the baseline’s remaining GSM8K errors.

The reason it stays out of the paper list for now is the architectural boundary. The abstract and HTML describe state flowing between decoder forward passes and positions, with extra inference passes used for per-token deliberation. That is adjacent to looped transformers, but it reads more like latent state carried across decoder invocations or repeated forward passes than a clean reused internal block within one forward process. A deeper read could change this if the authors specify a same-forward operator reuse mechanism that is central to the model.

## Other near-misses

- **Being-H0.7** uses learnable latent queries and future-informed training for VLA policies. It is useful context for latent world/action modeling, but the abstract does not describe repeated reuse of a learned operator inside one prediction.
- **LaST-R1** optimizes adaptive physical latent Chain-of-Thought for VLA control. It is a latent-reasoning/control paper, but not clearly a loop-model architecture under the repo rule.
- **Latent-GRPO** improves reinforcement learning over latent reasoning paths. It targets the optimization of latent traces rather than a recurrent-depth, DEQ, or shared-operator model.

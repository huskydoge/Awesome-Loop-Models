# Daily Loop-Model Watch - 2026-06-30

Two high-confidence papers were added today. Both are language-model papers, but they hit different parts of the loop-model boundary: one uses recurrent-depth Transformers to make latent reasoning competitive with explicit CoT, while the other uses recurrent unrolling as a way to recover capability after aggressive depth compression.

## Bridging the Gap Between Latent and Explicit Reasoning with Looped Transformers

LOTUS starts from a practical weakness of latent chain-of-thought methods: they can be faster than decoded reasoning, but have tended to fall behind explicit CoT as models scale. The paper's answer is a looped padded Transformer that processes `K` latent blocks in parallel for `R` recurrent iterations, with per-latent-position supervision against gold CoT-step tokens.

Why it was added: the abstract explicitly identifies looped, recurrent-depth Transformers as the mechanism, and says those Transformers reuse weights to increase computation depth without adding parameters. That is the collection's core same-forward learned-operator reuse rule.

Reader takeaway: latent reasoning does not have to be a black-box speed hack. With looped recurrence plus direct token-level supervision on latent positions, the paper reports matching explicit CoT at 3B scale while cutting thought-phase latency by 2.5x-6.9x.

## CHERRY: Compressed Hierarchical Experts with Recurrent Representational Yield

CHERRY is framed as a set of compute-efficiency techniques for language models. The loop-model part is the depth-compression result: a 48-layer, 1B-parameter Transformer is compressed to 6 layers, then restored through learned recurrent unrolling to 34 effective recurrent layers. The paper also combines compressed models as efficient experts.

Why it was added: recurrent unrolling is central to the reported depth recovery, and the abstract presents it as a learned mechanism for regaining effective depth after compression rather than as a training loop or repeated full-model call.

Reader takeaway: this is a useful efficiency-side data point for looped language models. The reported loss is close to a larger dense model while using fewer parameters, though the evidence is explicitly limited to one Korean model family and loss-based evaluation.

## Checked But Not Added

- **CoLT: Teaching Multi-Modal Models to Think with Chain of Latent Thoughts** (`2606.31986`) is a strong latent-reasoning paper, but the abstract describes chains of latent thought representations and supervisory decoders rather than a looped/reused internal learned block.
- **FR-DETR** (`2606.30471`) includes a Recurrent Focus Refinement Module for object detection under adverse weather. It is adjacent, but the abstract only says features are iteratively refined using coarse predictions as guidance; it does not make shared learned-operator reuse explicit enough for this collection.
- **Flow Reasoning Models** (`2606.29150`) uses discrete flow denoising, self-conditioning, and dynamically stable candidates as verifiers for structured reasoning. It is close in fixed-point language, but the loop is flow/denoising inference plus candidate search, not a learned recurrent-depth block reused as the model architecture.
- **CARVE** (`2606.27229`) remains a near-miss from the prior watch: it improves recurrent linear attention over sequence-time memory, not same-forward depth reuse.

# Daily Loop-Model Briefing - 2026-06-22

## Outcome

No new paper was added to the main collection today. The latest arXiv listings still stop at the Friday, June 19 announcement, and the strongest fresh signals were adjacent rather than in scope: theory about Chain-of-Thought computation, a broad architecture that can express recurrence, and feedback-aware conditional flows.

## Strongest watchlist signals

### Efficiently Representing Algorithms With Chain-of-Thought Transformers

This paper asks whether Chain-of-Thought Transformers can simulate Word RAM algorithms efficiently, rather than only Turing machines. It also studies continuous CoT and a hybrid setting with Transformer layers above a recurrent linear-RNN layer.

Why it was not added: the central compute loop is the generated reasoning trace or a theory construction around CoT simulation. The abstract does not present a looped, weight-tied, fixed-point, or reused internal learned block as the model mechanism to collect.

### ITNet: A Learnable Integral Transform That Subsumes Convolution, Attention, and Recurrence

ITNet proposes a learnable integral-transform architecture whose kernel can express convolution, self-attention, and autoregressive recurrence as special cases. The result is a broad unifying operator view with shared components across modalities.

Why it was not added: recurrence is discussed as an expressible special case, not as a same-forward recurrent-depth or implicit-layer mechanism. The paper is interesting architecture theory, but it does not cross the repo's conservative reused-module intake boundary.

### FlowBender: Feedback-Aware Training for Self-Correcting Conditional Flows

FlowBender trains conditional diffusion and flow models to consume their own alignment error during refinement. It uses look-ahead estimates and task-specific deviations to correct generation under image-to-image, restoration, and texturing constraints.

Why it was not added: the feedback loop is a conditional flow or diffusion-style refinement procedure. The repository excludes diffusion or energy-style iterative inference unless the paper's central contribution is a reused learned internal loop-model operator.

## Already covered

### Recurrent neural networks approximate continuous functions

This remains the clearest strict-scope signal in the Friday batch: a single fixed-weight recurrent network can improve approximation accuracy by running longer. It is already in the collection, so no duplicate entry was added today.

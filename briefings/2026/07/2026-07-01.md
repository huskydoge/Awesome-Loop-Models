# Daily Loop-Model Watch - 2026-07-01

One high-confidence paper was added today. The useful signal is not just that the paper studies latent reasoning; it directly builds on looped Transformers and proposes a new recurrence channel design for keeping multi-hop intermediate structure available across loop steps.

## DiscoLoop: Looping Discrete Embeddings and Continuous Hidden States for Multi-hop Reasoning

DiscoLoop studies two-hop reasoning where a model must compose parametric knowledge within a single forward pass instead of externalizing every step as chain-of-thought. The paper argues that standard non-recurrent Transformers lose useful first-hop facts across depth, while looped Transformers help by reusing memory but still suffer from representational mismatch between a decodable bridge entity and its token embedding.

Why it was added: the abstract explicitly frames Looped Transformers as the baseline mechanism and proposes a looping architecture whose recurrence carries both a discrete embedding channel and a continuous hidden-state channel. That makes the repeated learned computation part of the model's forward process, not an external prompting, search, or agent loop.

Reader takeaway: the mixed-channel recurrence nearly solves the symbolic and synthetic-language multi-hop setting with fewer training steps, and the paper reports lower training loss plus stronger benchmark performance than looped-transformer baselines in real-world pretraining.
